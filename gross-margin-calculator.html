What is gross margin?

Gross margin is the percentage of revenue left after subtracting COGS. It shows how efficiently you produce or deliver what you sell, before operating expenses like rent, salaries, and marketing.

Gross margin formula

Gross Margin (%) = (Revenue − COGS) / Revenue × 100

Example

If revenue is $10,000 and COGS is $6,500, gross profit is $3,500 and gross margin is 35%.

Gross margin vs markup

Margin is based on revenue. Markup is based on cost. Use our Markup Calculator and the guide Margin vs Markup to avoid pricing mistakes.
