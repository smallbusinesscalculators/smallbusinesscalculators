Contribution Margin Formula

Contribution Margin (per unit) = Price per Unit − Variable Cost per Unit
Contribution Margin % = (Contribution Margin ÷ Price per Unit) × 100

Contribution margin focuses only on price and variable cost. It does not subtract fixed costs directly. Instead, it shows how much each unit contributes toward covering those fixed costs.

Example Contribution Margin Calculation

Imagine you sell a product for $100 and the variable cost per unit is $60.

  • Price per Unit: $100
  • Variable Cost per Unit: $60
  • Contribution Margin: $40
  • Contribution Margin %: 40%

This means each sale contributes $40 toward fixed costs and profit, and 40% of the selling price remains after variable costs.

What Is Contribution Margin?

Contribution margin is the amount of revenue left after subtracting variable costs from a sale. It is one of the most important metrics for understanding unit economics and sales profitability.

Businesses use contribution margin to decide whether prices are high enough, whether products are worth promoting, and how long it will take to break even.

Why Contribution Margin Matters

  • It helps determine break-even point.
  • It helps evaluate pricing decisions.
  • It shows how profitable each unit sold really is.
  • It helps compare products, services, or offers.
  • It improves forecasting and financial planning.

Contribution Margin vs Profit Margin

Contribution margin and profit margin are related, but they measure different things. Contribution margin looks only at price minus variable cost. Profit margin looks at profit relative to revenue and may include more costs.

That means a business can have a strong contribution margin but still low overall profit if fixed costs are high. To compare this more fully, use the Profit Margin Calculator.

Tips for Improving Contribution Margin

  • Increase your selling price where the market allows it.
  • Reduce variable costs through better sourcing or efficiency.
  • Focus on high-margin products or services.
  • Bundle offers strategically to improve unit economics.
  • Track contribution margin regularly as costs and pricing change.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate contribution margin?

Contribution margin per unit is calculated as Price per Unit − Variable Cost per Unit. Contribution margin percentage is Contribution Margin ÷ Price × 100.

What is contribution margin used for?

Contribution margin is used to determine break-even point and to show how much each sale contributes toward covering fixed costs and generating profit.

What is a good contribution margin?

A good contribution margin depends on the industry, price level, and cost structure. Higher contribution margins generally make it easier to reach break-even faster and improve profitability.

What is the difference between contribution margin and profit margin?

Contribution margin focuses on price minus variable cost. Profit margin looks at profit relative to revenue and can include more than variable costs.

Why is contribution margin important?

Contribution margin is important because it helps businesses understand pricing power, product profitability, and how quickly sales cover fixed costs.
