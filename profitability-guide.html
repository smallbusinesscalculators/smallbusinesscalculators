Profit Margin, Markup & Break-Even Explained

A practical profitability guide for small business owners.

If you run a small business, profitability is not theory. It determines whether you build equity, create cash flow, or slowly drain your own energy.

Sales matter. But profit builds stability.

What Is Profit?

Profit = Revenue – Costs

If you sell a product for $100 and it costs $60, your profit is $40. Simple — but problems usually come from misunderstanding which costs are included.

Gross Profit vs Net Profit

Gross Profit

Gross Profit = Revenue – Cost of Goods Sold

Gross profit shows whether your product pricing works.

Net Profit

Net Profit = Revenue – Total Expenses

Net profit shows whether your business model is sustainable.

Margin vs Markup

Profit Margin (Based on Revenue)

Profit Margin % = Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

Markup (Based on Cost)

Markup % = Profit ÷ Cost × 100

A 50% markup does NOT equal a 50% profit margin. Confusing them leads to underpricing.

Contribution Margin

Contribution Margin = Price – Variable Cost

Contribution margin shows how much each sale contributes toward covering fixed costs.

  • Contribution Margin Calculator

Break-Even Point

Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs ÷ Contribution Margin per Unit

Break-even defines your minimum survival sales level.

  • Break-Even Calculator

ROI (Return on Investment)

ROI % = (Return – Investment) ÷ Investment × 100

ROI measures efficiency of investments.

How These Metrics Work Together

  • Markup sets your price
  • Margin measures profitability
  • Contribution margin covers fixed costs
  • Break-even defines survival level
  • ROI measures efficiency
  • Net profit confirms sustainability

Common Small Business Pricing Mistakes

  • Confusing margin and markup
  • Setting prices based only on competitors
  • Ignoring fixed costs
  • Chasing revenue instead of profit
  • Growing sales without checking margins

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good profit margin for a small business?

Many healthy small businesses aim for 10–20% net margin or higher.

Is margin more important than markup?

Margin reflects performance. Markup helps set price. Both matter.

Why is break-even important?

It shows the minimum sales needed to avoid losses.
