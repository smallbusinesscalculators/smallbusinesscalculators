Net Profit Formula

Net Profit = Revenue − Total Expenses
Net Profit Margin % = (Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100

Net profit shows the money left over after every expense has been subtracted from revenue. Net profit margin shows that result as a percentage of revenue.

Example Net Profit Calculation

Imagine your business generates $10,000 in revenue and has $8,500 in total expenses.

  • Revenue: $10,000
  • Total Expenses: $8,500
  • Net Profit: $1,500
  • Net Profit Margin: 15%

This means the business keeps $1,500 after all expenses, and 15% of revenue remains as net profit.

What Is Net Profit?

Net profit, also called net income, is the amount left after subtracting all business expenses from total revenue. It is one of the clearest indicators of whether a business is truly profitable.

Unlike gross profit, which only subtracts direct costs, net profit includes all expenses such as rent, salaries, software, taxes, interest, utilities, and overhead. That makes it a more complete profitability measure.

Gross Profit vs Net Profit

Gross profit subtracts only cost of goods sold or direct costs. Net profit subtracts all expenses.

That is why a business can have a healthy gross profit but still end up with low net profit if overhead and operating costs are too high. To compare both, use the Profit Margin Calculator.

Why Net Profit Matters

  • It shows how much money the business actually keeps.
  • It helps measure overall business profitability.
  • It can reveal whether overhead or operating costs are too high.
  • It is important for budgeting, forecasting, and business planning.
  • It helps owners compare performance across different periods.

Tips for Improving Net Profit

  • Increase revenue through better pricing, sales volume, or product mix.
  • Reduce unnecessary overhead and operating expenses.
  • Track margins regularly, not just total revenue.
  • Review subscriptions, software, and fixed costs for savings opportunities.
  • Use break-even and ROI analysis together with net profit for better decisions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate net profit?

Net profit is calculated as Revenue − Total Expenses. Net profit margin is calculated as Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100.

What is the difference between gross profit and net profit?

Gross profit subtracts only direct costs or cost of goods sold. Net profit subtracts all expenses, including overhead, taxes, and operating costs.

What is a good net profit margin?

A good net profit margin depends on the industry, business model, and cost structure. Some businesses operate on lower margins, while software and service businesses may have higher margins.

Can net profit be negative?

Yes. If total expenses are greater than revenue, net profit will be negative, which means the business made a loss.

Why is net profit important?

Net profit shows how much money remains after all expenses. It is one of the most important numbers for measuring overall business performance.
