Margin vs Markup: What’s the Difference?

Margin and markup are not the same. Margin is based on revenue (selling price), while markup is based on cost. This guide explains the difference with formulas and examples so you can price correctly and avoid underpricing.

Use our Free Margin vs Markup Calculator to convert margin to markup or markup to margin instantly.

Margin is calculated as:
Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

Markup is calculated as:
Profit ÷ Cost × 100

The key difference is simple: margin is based on selling price, while markup is based on cost.

Margin
Profit ÷ Revenue
Markup
Profit ÷ Cost

Quick Definitions

  • Profit: Revenue − Cost
  • Margin (%): Profit ÷ Revenue × 100
  • Markup (%): Profit ÷ Cost × 100

Example (Same Numbers, Different Percentages)

Let’s say you sell an item for $100 and it costs you $60.

  • Profit = 100 − 60 = $40
  • Margin = 40 ÷ 100 = 40%
  • Markup = 40 ÷ 60 = 66.67%

This is why margin vs markup matters. The same product can have a 40% margin and a 66.67% markup at the same time. Confusing the two often leads to pricing mistakes.

How to Convert Margin to Markup and Markup to Margin

To convert margin to markup, use:

Markup % = (Margin % ÷ (100 − Margin %)) × 100

To convert markup to margin, use:

Margin % = (Markup % ÷ (100 + Markup %)) × 100

When to Use Margin vs Markup

  • Use margin when you start from selling price or want to measure profitability.
  • Use markup when you start from cost and want to set a selling price.
