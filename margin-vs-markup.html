Margin vs Markup: What’s the Difference?

Margin is calculated as:
Profit ÷ Revenue × 100

Markup is calculated as:
Profit ÷ Cost × 100

The key difference: margin is based on selling price, while markup is based on cost.

Margin and markup are not the same. Margin is based on revenue (selling price). Markup is based on cost. This guide explains both with formulas and examples so you don’t price products incorrectly.

Margin
Profit ÷ Revenue
Markup
Profit ÷ Cost

Quick Definitions

  • Profit: Revenue − Cost
  • Margin (%): Profit ÷ Revenue × 100
  • Markup (%): Profit ÷ Cost × 100

Example (Same Numbers, Different Percentages)

Let’s say you sell an item for $100 and it costs you $60.

  • Profit = 100 − 60 = $40
  • Margin = 40 ÷ 100 = 40%
  • Markup = 40 ÷ 60 = 66.67%

That’s why a “50% markup” does not mean “50% margin”.

When to Use Margin vs Markup

  • Use margin when you start from a selling price or want to measure profitability.
  • Use markup when you start from cost and want to set a selling price.

If you know your selling price, use the Profit Margin Calculator. If you know your cost and want a price, use the Markup Calculator.

Common Mistake: Pricing Too Low

Many businesses aim for a target margin but accidentally apply markup instead. That can produce a lower selling price than intended and reduce profitability.
