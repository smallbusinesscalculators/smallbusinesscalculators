What is Profit Margin?

Profit margin measures how much of your revenue remains as profit after covering costs. It shows how efficiently you turn sales into profit.

Profit Margin Formula

Profit = Revenue − Cost
Gross Margin % = Profit ÷ Revenue × 100
Markup % = Profit ÷ Cost × 100

Margin vs Markup – What's the Difference?

Margin is based on revenue. Markup is based on cost. A 50% markup does not equal a 50% margin.

What Is a Good Profit Margin?

A good profit margin depends on your industry, cost structure, and pricing strategy. Here are general benchmarks:

  • Retail: 5–20%
  • Service businesses: 10–30%
  • SaaS / Software: 60–80%
  • Ecommerce: 10–30%

Higher profit margins usually indicate better efficiency, stronger pricing power, or lower operating costs.

How to Increase Your Profit Margin

  • Increase prices strategically
  • Negotiate lower supplier costs
  • Reduce overhead and operational waste
  • Focus on higher-margin products

Example Calculation

If your revenue is $1,000 and your cost is $650:

  • Profit = $350
  • Margin = 35%
  • Markup = 53.85%

Notice how markup is always higher than margin when profit exists.
