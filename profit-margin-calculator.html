What is profit margin?

Profit margin measures how much of your revenue remains as profit after covering costs. It shows how efficiently you turn sales into profit.

Profit margin formulas

Profit = Revenue − Cost
Gross Margin % = Profit ÷ Revenue × 100
Markup % = Profit ÷ Cost × 100

Margin vs markup

Margin is based on revenue. Markup is based on cost. A 50% markup does not equal a 50% margin. Learn the difference in our guide: Margin vs Markup.

What is a good profit margin?

A good profit margin depends on your industry, cost structure, and pricing strategy. Here are general benchmarks:

  • Retail: 5–20%
  • Service businesses: 10–30%
  • SaaS / Software: 60–80%
  • Ecommerce: 10–30%

Example calculation

If your revenue is $1,000 and your cost is $650:

  • Profit = $350
  • Margin = 35%
  • Markup = 53.85%

Frequently Asked Questions

How do you calculate profit margin?

Profit margin (%) = (Revenue − Cost) ÷ Revenue × 100.

What is the difference between margin and markup?

Margin uses revenue as the denominator. Markup uses cost as the denominator.

What is a good profit margin for a small business?

It depends on industry. Retail often runs 5–20%, services 10–30%, ecommerce 10–30%, and SaaS can be higher.

How do you calculate gross profit?

Gross profit = Revenue − Cost.
