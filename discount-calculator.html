How to Use This Discount Calculator

Enter the original price and the discount percentage. Then click calculate to see the discount amount and the final price after the discount.

  1. Enter the original price.
  2. Enter the discount percentage.
  3. Click Calculate.
  4. Review the discount amount and final price.

This is useful for shopping, pricing promotions, comparing deals, and checking sale prices quickly.

Discount Formula

Discount Amount = Original Price × Discount %
Final Price = Original Price − Discount Amount
Savings % = (Discount Amount ÷ Original Price) × 100

The discount amount tells you how much is taken off the original price, and the final price tells you what you actually pay.

Example Discount Calculation

Imagine the original price is $200 and the discount is 15%.

  • Original Price: $200
  • Discount: 15%
  • Discount Amount: $30
  • Final Price: $170

This means you save $30 and pay $170 after the discount.

What Is a Discount?

A discount is a reduction in the original price of a product or service. It is usually shown as a percentage, such as 10%, 20%, or 50% off.

Discount calculations are useful for shopping, promotions, pricing strategy, and checking whether a deal is really as good as it looks.

Why a Discount Calculator Is Useful

  • It helps you calculate sale prices quickly.
  • It makes it easier to compare deals.
  • It reduces mistakes in percentage calculations.
  • It is useful for shopping, ecommerce, and promotions.
  • It helps businesses plan discounts more clearly.

Common Discount Examples

  • 10% off $100 = $90 final price
  • 20% off $50 = $40 final price
  • 25% off $200 = $150 final price
  • 50% off $80 = $40 final price
